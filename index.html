I am a quantitative researcher with over 7 years of experience conducting research in both academia and industry. My forte is formulating clear and testable hypotheses and testing them using statistical tools, such as regression analysis.

I am currently finishing my PhD at the Human-Computer Interaction Institute at Carnegie Mellon University. Advised by Jason Hong and Robert Kraut, I closely study how social interactions play a role in subjective well-being. Specifically, my research applies mobile sensing to collect behavioral data on smartphones. These data can predict when social interactions happen and what medium they are carried out. This powerful method provides an abundance of data, on which I apply statistical models to understand the role of social interactions in one's well-being.
